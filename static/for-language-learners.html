Why a regular dictionary stunts your learning.

When you're learning a language, the dictionary is your best friend and your worst enemy. You encounter a new word, you open a dictionary app, and you are presented with a list of eight different possible meanings. You scan the list, guess which one applies to your sentence, and move on.

The problem is twofold. First, guessing the correct sense of a word out of eight options is incredibly difficult when you are already struggling with the grammar of the sentence. Second, regular dictionaries give you a sterile, isolated definition. They do not tell you why the author chose that word, or what subtle nuance it carries in that specific context.

Lexio flips the model. Instead of giving you a list of every possible thing a word could mean, it uses advanced AI (Gemini 2.5 Flash or Claude Sonnet 4.5) to read the sentence and tell you exactly what the word does mean right there. It handles the idioms, the phrasal verbs, and the poetic metaphors that traditional dictionaries miss.

And because language acquisition requires repetition, Lexio doesn't let your lookups disappear. Every word is saved with its surrounding sentence, ready to be exported directly into your Anki spaced-repetition deck. Reading practice becomes vocabulary building, automatically.
