The problem

A dictionary doesn't know which meaning you need.

Most words carry several meanings. A dictionary hands you all of them — a list to sort through, mid-sentence, while the one that actually fits slips away. So you guess, break your flow to look it up, or skip the word and lose what the writer meant.

A normal dictionary

Every definition of a word — generic and out of context. You're left deciding which sense the author intended.

Lexio

Reads the sentence around the word and gives you the one meaning that fits right here — and why the author chose it.

For educators

Turn reading assignments into active immersion.

Lexio keeps students inside the native text. Instead of pasting a paragraph into a bulk translator, they read it as written — and tap only the words that block comprehension. Every lookup is a deliberate act of learning, not a shortcut around it.

From daily reading to exam-ready review

Every word a student saves lands in their Word Bank, kept with the exact sentence it came from. One click exports the whole set to Anki — so a week of reading assignments becomes a spaced-repetition deck, built automatically and ready before the exam.

Nuance, idioms, and origins — without hallucination

Balanced mode runs on Gemini 2.5 Flash and Deep mode on Claude Sonnet 4.5. They read the whole sentence before defining a single word, so students get the meaning the author intended — including idioms and register. The Etymology view traces each word to its real roots, grounding every definition in linguistic history rather than inventing one.

in Greek tragedy

Not anger — a heavier, more clinical despair, the modern condition of urban exhaustion.

The capacity to remain in uncertainty without "irritable reaching after fact and reason."
